# Add Attributes to Help Increase Your Shop's Visibility (Etsy 官方属性指南)

Source: https://www.etsy.com/seller-handbook/article/604203126614
(Etsy Seller Handbook, dated August 26, 2025; fetched 2026-06-12. Full article content below.)

Learn how to add listing attributes, an important factor in helping items appear in relevant Etsy searches.

On Etsy, attributes play a distinct role in powering category-specific filters that connect relevant listings to relevant shoppers. Collecting this structured data across the millions of listings helps provide a better search and browse experience for buyers, and typically results in more visits and ultimately sales for sellers.

## What are attributes and how do they impact Etsy search?

Attributes are additional types of information sellers should provide about their products while creating or editing a listing to help describe what they're selling so that buyers can easily find just the right item. Attributes differ from open-field listing tags because they're **limited and category-specific**. They're created by Etsy based on extensive research into buyer search behaviors. Because attribute options are limited to a structured set of data, like a fixed list of color options or types of gemstones, they can be surfaced to buyers across different types of shopping experiences in a more systematic way.

The words and phrases that appear in attributes can help a listing match with a shopper's query in relevant Etsy search results. Attributes are also used to create **category-specific filters**, which buyers can use to narrow down their results while shopping (the filters in the sidebar of search results). **The only way for a listing to appear in filtered search results is if it contains that attribute.** Providing accurate attribute information also allows Etsy to show your listings to buyers in more places across the site, including relevant recipient- or season-specific shopping experiences and targeted marketing initiatives.

Etsy's Taxonomy team uses search data, buyer interviews, merchandise research, and analytics to discover what shoppers want to know about items on Etsy. Attributes are often introduced first within popular, listing-dense categories where buyers may be overwhelmed by the volume of search results.

The attribute options a seller sees vary based on which category they've chosen for that listing. **Most categories include attributes for color, holiday, and occasion.** Beyond that, the attributes differ depending on the category, surfacing the most relevant information buyers want to know.

Official examples:
- A seller lists in the **Oil Paintings** category → attribute options for orientation (horizontal or vertical) and subject matter.
- A seller lists in the **Engagement Rings** category → options for ring size, gemstone, and setting.
- A seller lists a vintage dress in the **Dresses** category → options including material and style.

## How do you add attributes effectively?

You can add or update a listing's attributes during the listing process or using the listing editor. Key rules:

- **Add all the attributes that are relevant** to your listing, and **skip any that don't apply**. Adding irrelevant attributes leads to a disappointing experience for shoppers — if they see items that aren't relevant to their search or filter, they're unlikely to buy, or even trust Etsy in the future.
- **Attributes should describe what a product IS, not how it's used.** For example, the Father's Day "Holiday" attribute is appropriate for a Father's Day card, but shouldn't be used on a wallet that might make a good Father's Day gift. (Use *tags* like "dad gift" for the gifting angle instead.)
- Add attributes even when they're less precise than your own wording — if "pink" is accurate for your "magenta" yarn, add the Pink color attribute too.
- If you don't see attribute options within your listing category, don't worry — about half of Etsy categories have at least one category-specific attribute, and new ones are added on a rolling basis.

Remember: your tags, titles, categories, and attributes all work together to match your listings with shoppers' searches.
