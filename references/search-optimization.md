## How Etsy search ranks listings (mechanics worth knowing)

- Etsy search takes a **holistic view** of the listing — title, tags, attributes, description, **first photo**, reviews, and more — when matching items with buyers. The title doesn't need to do all the work; spread key details across tags, description, and attributes.
- Keywords across titles, descriptions, tags, categories, and attributes drive **query matching (phase 1)** of ranking; keywords and phrases in the **listing description** are considered in the **second phase** of search ranking.
- **Word order in the title has no direct effect on ranking** — but listings that are popular with shoppers perform better in search, so a scannable title that earns clicks improves ranking *over time* (popularity feedback loop). Put the most important keywords first for the shopper's sake, not the algorithm's.
- **Customer service quality is a ranking factor**: average review rating, message response rate, and case rate from the past three months are included in search ranking. Shops with clear policies get a positive search-placement effect, and using Etsy's shop policies template gives an additional boost.
- **Shipping price affects US search placement**: US domestic listings with shipping prices under $6 are prioritized (with some exceptions). Etsy's own data shows shifting some shipping cost into the item price (same total) improves sales.
- New listings appear in search even with zero reviews, as long as there are no listing/shop quality issues.

## Title rules (official key tips)

- **Clearly name the item for sale** ("mug", "dress", "lantern") — shoppers only see the first few words in search results.
- **Put the most important traits upfront**: color, material, size.
- **Keep it under ~15 words**; streamline for clarity and scannability.
- **Only include holidays, occasions, or recipient types if they're essential to what the item IS** — "birthday candle" or "Halloween costume" yes; a generic item that merely *makes* a good gift, no (use tags for that angle).
- **Move subjective words ("perfect", "beautiful") to the description.**
- **Try not to repeat words** within the title.
- **Don't add price, shipping, or sale info** — Etsy badges items in search automatically.

> Implication for this skill: the old habit of stuffing a title with 4–5 comma-separated keyword phrases up to 140 chars conflicts with current official guidance. Prefer a shorter, readable title (~15 words) that names the item and its key traits, and push recipient/occasion phrases into the 13 tags.

## Tags: the Google rule of thumb

Each tag is an opportunity to appear in a search; unused or irrelevant tags are missed opportunities. Always add 13. Tags should be **multi-word phrases in natural-sounding language**.

> **Official rule of thumb: "If you can't imagine someone typing a phrase into Google, it shouldn't be in your tags."**

Add variety: describe **what the product is, how it's made, and who it's for**.

## Titles & tags must be in the shop language

Titles and tags should be in the language chosen when the shop was opened. **Adding tags in a different language won't help listings appear in search** — Etsy auto-translates titles and tags into the shopper's language. Sellers who speak multiple languages should tag in their native shop language and optionally add their own translations.

(For this skill: this is why copy-paste blocks are single-language — match the user's shop language, English by default.)

## First photo SEO

The first listing image is what gets the click from search results:

- **Avoid text overlays, collages, or composite images for the first thumbnail** — they hurt search optimization.
- First photo must be at least **635 × 635 px** to avoid ranking lower in searches; Etsy recommends **2000 px+** on each side.
- A clean, simple background keeps focus on the product (white-background POD renders are fine as photo #1).
- Up to **20 photos** per listing; more photos → more buyer information → better conversion. Suggested follow-up shots: multiple angles, close-ups of details/texture, lifestyle/in-context images (product in a patio, on a model, etc.), natural light, no flash.

## Description ranking tips (beyond Keywords 101)

- Put **essential information at the top** (sizes, dimensions, colors, ordering directions); end with the product's story in the brand's voice.
- Incorporate relevant keywords in the first few sentences; never paste the title verbatim or dump a keyword list.
- Use bullet points or short paragraphs — a wall of text is intimidating.
- Tell a story: inspiration or process behind the product builds buyer connection (POD shops can describe the design concept).

## Attribute specifics (supplements attributes.md)

Attributes describe characteristics like material, primary color, secondary color, holiday, occasion, height, and width. Deeper categories unlock **category-specific attributes** (necklace → chain style, number of strands; beads → size and shape). **Choose the closest available option even if it's not exactly how you'd describe it.** If a phrase appears in your attributes, don't repeat it as a tag.

## Ongoing keyword maintenance (for advising the user)

- Search optimization is not "set it and forget it": refresh tags/titles on low-traffic listings, especially **at the start of a new season or before a major holiday**.
- Scan listings for tags with lots of repeated phrases — those slots are opportunities for new tags.
- The **Etsy search visibility page** in Shop Manager (and the Seller app) shows shop / listing / customer-service quality insights and suggests actions; Etsy also offers an optional AI title-suggestion tool there for eligible English listings.
- A **shop title** (tagline or concise description of what you sell) also influences SEO.
